Case Study · K-8 Science

Honey Bees & Beehives — built to wonder

How a discovery-led K-8 module hit 4.7/5 teacher ratings.

Client

K-8 Curriculum Publisher

Audience

Grades 4–6 (ages 9–12) · 32,000 learners pilot cohort

My Role

Lead instructional designer, Storyline & illustration coordination

Tools

Articulate Storyline 360, Vyond, Audacity, Illustrator

Timeline

8 weeks

4.7Teacher Rating /5
96%Student Completion
+27%Post-test Lift

The brief

The publisher had a 12-page textbook chapter on bees. Students hated it. Teachers asked for a "make this interactive" treatment with the same content and no extra teacher prep.

The approach: discovery learning

Instead of "here are the parts of a hive — read", we built a virtual beehive students could click around to discover the parts themselves. Every interaction came with a tiny prediction question first: "What do you think a worker bee does?"

  • 4 stops in a 3D-ish beehive (drawn in Illustrator, layered in Storyline)
  • 1 mini-game: pollinate 5 flowers in 30 seconds
  • 1 narrative: a worker bee's day, told as a comic
  • 1 reflection: build your own field-notes journal

The solution

A 25-minute exploratory course with no linear progress bar — students chose the order. Built-in checkpoints meant teachers still got measurable data.

The results

  • 96% completion in classroom pilots
  • 4.7/5 teacher rating — adopted by 6 districts in first semester
  • Post-test scores up 27% vs. textbook-only control group
  • "Best science thing I've ever used" — actual 5th-grader quote, paraphrased

My class genuinely groaned when the bell rang. Never thought I'd say that about a science lesson on bees.
