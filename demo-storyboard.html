Storyboard Demo

A real storyboard in action

5 slides from my Fire Safety in Hospital project — annotated for SMEs, devs, and reviewers.

What is a storyboard?

A storyboard is a screen-by-screen blueprint of a course — what learners see, hear, and do. It's signed off before any Storyline development begins. Skip this and you'll rebuild your course three times.

Slide 01 · Welcome

On-screen: Welcome to Fire Safety Training. By the end of this 30-min module you'll know the RACE protocol.

Narration: Welcome. In the next 30 minutes you'll learn the four-step RACE protocol every hospital employee must know.

Visuals: Hero image: hospital corridor with subtle motion. Logo bottom-right.

Interactions: Click Start to begin

Note: Set narration volume default 80%

Slide 02 · Course Map

On-screen: 3 modules · 12 screens · 1 assessment

Narration: [no audio — visual only]

Visuals: Branching visual showing all 3 modules as connected nodes.

Interactions: Click any node to jump to that module

Note: Highlight current node on hover

Slide 03 · Why RACE Matters

On-screen: Last year, fire incidents in US hospitals injured 1,500 people. Most could have been prevented with a 4-step routine.

Narration: Hospital fires aren't rare — but trained responses make all the difference. Here's why RACE matters.

Visuals: B-roll video of news headlines (3 sec). Stat counter animation.

Interactions: Continue button appears at 6 seconds

Note: Caption track required (WCAG 1.2.2)

Slide 04 · R = Rescue

On-screen: Rescue anyone in immediate danger. Move them to a safe horizontal zone.

Narration: R stands for Rescue. Your first action is to move people from immediate danger to a safe horizontal zone.

Visuals: Diagram of horizontal compartments with patient icons moving along arrows.

Interactions: Click hotspots on diagram to reveal protocols

Note: Provide alt text for diagram (WCAG 1.1.1)

Slide 05 · Knowledge Check

On-screen: Which is the first step in the RACE protocol?

Narration: [no narration — assessment]

Visuals: Multiple-choice question UI. 4 options. Submit + retry buttons.

Interactions: Click an answer → Submit

Note: Pass = 80%. Show feedback layer on submit.
