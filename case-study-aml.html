Case Study · Banking

Anti-Money Laundering training that front-line staff loved

How a branching-scenario approach lifted compliance scores by 34%.

Client

Global Retail Bank

Audience

4,500 front-line branch staff across 8 countries

My Role

Lead instructional designer, Storyline developer, localisation lead

Tools

Articulate Storyline 360, Vyond, Audacity, Veracity LRS

Languages

EN · ES · PT · FR · DE · AR (RTL) · HI · ZH

Timeline

12 weeks · 3 sprints

94%Completion Rate
+34%Score Improvement
−40%Seat Time

The brief

The bank's existing 90-minute compliance course had a 62% pass rate, terrible reviews, and a CFO running out of patience. They needed something staff would actually finish — and would actually change behaviour at the teller window.

The approach

We threw out the chapter structure and rebuilt around a single branching scenario: "Maria's Suspicious Customer." Learners step into Maria's role at the teller window, make decisions, and see consequences ripple through the day.

  • Started with learner interviews — 12 tellers across 4 markets
  • Mapped 6 real-world money-laundering red flags from compliance team
  • Wrote 1 master story with 3 branching paths and 7 decision points
  • Storyline 360 + custom JS for variable state across the scenario
  • Locale-specific characters and currency in every translation

The solution

A 50-minute branching scenario course replacing the 90-minute click-through. Real conversations. Real consequences. Real red flags.

Built-in micro-assessments after each decision give learners feedback in context — not 20 minutes later in a multiple-choice gauntlet.

The results

  • Completion rate climbed from 62% → 94%
  • Post-course assessment scores up 34%
  • Seat time down 40% (50 min vs. 90 min)
  • 6-month behaviour audit: 28% increase in correctly logged Suspicious Activity Reports
  • xAPI dashboards in Power BI let the compliance team spot risky markets in real time

Madhu's branching scenario was the first compliance course our staff voluntarily replayed. The 28% lift in correctly-logged SARs is the kind of behaviour change we used to call impossible.

KM
Kristen Miller
Senior Compliance Manager · Global Bank

Lessons learned

  • Branching narrative beat 12 modules — once.
  • Locale-specific characters mattered more than translation quality.
  • Storyboard sign-off saved us 4 weeks of rework. Worth every hour.
